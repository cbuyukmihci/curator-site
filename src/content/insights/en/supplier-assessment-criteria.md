Major industrial customers are applying increasingly comprehensive assessment processes in supplier selection. In these processes, not only product quality but also operational maturity, documentation discipline, and compliance structure play critical roles.

## Core Assessment Dimensions

### 1. Operational Competence
Production capacity, process repeatability, and quality control mechanisms are the first areas evaluated. Customers measure the supplier's ability to deliver consistent quality on time.

### 2. Documentation and Traceability
ISO certifications, process documentation, and traceability records are now among basic expectations. Missing or outdated documentation is perceived as a serious risk signal.

### 3. Information Security Structure
Especially in defense and technology sectors, suppliers' information security management systems (ISO 27001) and data protection compliance (KVKK/GDPR) are being questioned.

### 4. Financial Stability
The supplier's financial sustainability is the guarantee of a long-term business relationship for major customers.

## Preparation Recommendations

- Evaluate your current processes with an independent perspective
- Proactively close documentation gaps
- Strengthen your compliance structure
- Make your financial reporting more transparent
